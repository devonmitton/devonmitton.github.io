I believe design is more than how it looks

  • How it looks
  • How it works
  • How it fails
  • How it feels
  • How it affects

I began my career as a web designer, and I quickly learned HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I discovered that the product was better when “how it looks” meshed with “how it works”; It made the product feel seamless and it didn't feel clunky.

I learned we can design how a product feels.

Since then I’ve approached design with a holistic view that starts by thinking of the outcomes, and working backwards from there by asking questions like “How do we want people thinking and feeling after they’ve used it, and how to we go about creating that for them?”.

Because people often think about design as “how it looks”, I describe myself as a user experience designer to better communicate the holistic concept. Although, UX has become a bit of a buzzword lately.

So, I define a user’s experience as every point of impression or interaction that a person has with an organization, their products or services which impacts how they think and feel about that organization. I don’t think that definition describes the role of a single “user experience designer”. I think that ultimately it’s everyone’s job within an organization to create delightful interactions for those users and customers.

My family gets my first and best

I love what I do, and I sometimes do it as a hobby. I will always meet deadlines and sometimes I will work extra to ensure that I will. But work is not the center of my life, it’s how I try to provide the best living for my family.

While I am flexible with my time to ensure I get done what I promised to get done, I may at times need some flexibility to drop off or pick up kids from school, have doctors appointments or take a look at the cool picture they just made for me.

So while I love what I do, I love my family more.

I challenge everything

I’m not trying to be a jerk, I’m just trying to understand the “why”. I think debates are good as long as they’re not about ego because it forces the best idea to win, and I’m always on the look-out to equip myself with the best ideas.

I’ve been told by many people that I’m stubborn. I do have very strong opinions, but they are loosely held. This is why I challenge everything. I’m always looking for better ways to do things, so I’m always testing, researching, and tinkering. When a better idea comes along and it survives the testing and researching, I will adopt it instantly. But I’m not satisfied to do things just because that’s how they’ve been done. I need to understand “why”.
