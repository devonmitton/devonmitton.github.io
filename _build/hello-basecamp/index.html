I believe design is more than how it looks

  • How it looks
  • How it works
  • How it fails
  • How it feels
  • How it affects

I began my career as a web (ui) designer, and I quickly learned HTML/CSS and JavaScript. My first job was working with Flash and creating “interactive experiences” and I discovered that the product is better when “how it looks” meshes with “how it works”. I learned that I was able to make better designs when I understood the code behind the scenes. I found the product seemed to mesh better; it wasn’t clunky or disjointed. It created a “how it feels”.

Since then I’ve approached design with a holistic view that starts by thinking of the outcomes, and working backwards from there by asking questions like “someone uses this thing, then they go on with their day. How do we want them thinking and feeling after they’ve used it, and how to we go about creating that for them?”. Because people often think about Design as “how it looks”, I’ve been calling myself a “user experience designer” to describe my approach.

I define a user’s experience as every point of impression or interaction a person has with an organization, their products or services which impacts how they think and feel about that organization. I don’t think that’s the role of a single “user experience designer”. However, I think that’s everyone’s job to create delightful interactions with those users and customers. I think everyone is designing something from their eloquence, modularity, and simplicity in code to all the considerations into how people think about and interact with buttons and interfaces, down to well-crafted elements of copy.
