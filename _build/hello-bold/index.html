I think design is more than how it looks.

  • How it looks
  • How it works
  • How it fails
  • How it feels
  • How it affects

I started out as a web designer. Then I learned back-end programming and discovered that the product is better when “how it looks” meshes with “how it works”. It creates a “how it feels”, that isn’t clunky or disjointed.

So I’ve started to take on a wholistic view of design that starts by thinking of the outcomes, and working backwards from there.

A user’s experience is every point of interaction a person has with an organization, their products or services, which impacts how they think and feel about that organization. A user experience designer is responsible for creating an environment where the best and most delightful interactions can happen.

I love to noodle out ideas with a pen and paper, work in Sketch to create layouts and design patterns, build them in HTML/CSS/JavaScript, and even dive into the server-side code and work with data structures. But I’m most at home designing UI and building them with HTML/CSS/JavaScript.

I challenge everything.

I’m not trying to be a jerk, I’m just trying to understand why. I think debates are good because it forces the best idea to win, and as long as the debates are not about ego. I’ve been told by many people that I’m stubborn.

I do have very strong opinions, but they are loosely held. This is why I challenge everything. I’m always looking for better ways to do things, so I’m always testing, researching, and tinkering. When a better idea comes along and it survives the testing and researching, I will adopt it instantly. But I’m not satisfied to do things just because thats how they’ve been done.
